      The brain is an organ that serves as the center of the nervous system in all vertebrate and most invertevrate animals. The brain is located in the head, usually close to the sensory organs for senses such as vision.

      The brain is the most complex organ in a vertebrate's body.

      In a human, the cerebral cortex contains approximately 10-20 billion neurons, and the estimatedd number of neurons in the cerebellum is 55-70 billion neurons.

      Each neuron is connected by synapes to several thousand other neurons. Thses neurons communicate with one another by means of long protoplasmic fibers called axons, which carry trains of signal pulses called action potentials to distant parts of the brain or body targeting specific recipient cells.
